Explore Tuscany

Make the most of your trip to this beautiful region

Day Trips

Tuscany has so much to offer. Here are some of our favourite places to explore.

Florence

The birthplace of the Renaissance. Visit the Uffizi Gallery, climb the Duomo, stroll across Ponte Vecchio, and get lost in the charming streets. About 1 hour from La Conca.

Siena

A stunning medieval city famous for its shell-shaped Piazza del Campo and the magnificent Duomo. Don't miss the views from Torre del Mangia. About 1.5 hours away.

Arezzo

A charming hilltop town with Etruscan origins. Famous for its antique market and the setting of "Life is Beautiful". Just 30 minutes from La Conca.

Cortona

The hilltop town made famous by "Under the Tuscan Sun". Wander the steep streets, enjoy stunning views, and savour the slow pace of Italian life. About 45 minutes away.

San Gimignano

Known as the "Manhattan of the Middle Ages" for its medieval towers. Sample the world-famous gelato and enjoy panoramic Tuscan views. About 1.5 hours away.

Sansepolcro

The nearest town to La Conca! Home to Piero della Francesca's masterpieces. A lovely authentic Italian town with great restaurants and shops. Just 10 minutes away.

Wine & Food

Tuscany is a food and wine lover's paradise. Here's how to make the most of it.

Wine Tasting

The region is famous for Chianti, Brunello di Montalcino, and Vino Nobile di Montepulciano. Many wineries offer tastings and tours - we're happy to recommend our favourites.

Tip: Book vineyard visits in advance, especially in May!

Cooking Classes

Learn to make fresh pasta, traditional rag√π, or Tuscan bread. Many agriturismos and cooking schools offer hands-on classes in beautiful settings.

Tip: Great activity for the day before or after the wedding!

Local Specialities

Try bistecca alla fiorentina (Florentine steak), pici pasta, ribollita soup, and cantucci biscuits with Vin Santo. Every town has its own delicious traditions.

Tip: Lunch is often better value than dinner at top restaurants.

Activities

Beyond sightseeing, there's plenty to keep you entertained.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

See Tuscany from above at sunrise. An unforgettable experience floating over the rolling hills, vineyards, and medieval villages.

Vespa Tours

Explore the countryside like a local on a classic Italian Vespa. Guided tours take you along scenic routes through vineyards and olive groves.

Thermal Spas

Tuscany has natural hot springs. Relax at Bagno Vignoni, Saturnia, or Bagni San Filippo - perfect for recovering from the wedding celebrations!

Cycling

The Tuscan hills offer incredible cycling routes. Rent bikes locally or join a guided tour through the Val d'Orcia or Chianti regions.

Truffle Hunting

Join a local truffle hunter and their trained dogs to search for these prized delicacies, followed by a truffle-themed lunch.

Art & Museums

From the Uffizi to hidden gems in small towns, Tuscany is an art lover's dream. Pre-book tickets for popular museums to skip the queues.

Where to Eat

We'll share our personal restaurant recommendations closer to the wedding. In the meantime, here are some tips.

What time do Italians eat? +

Lunch is typically 12:30-2:30pm, and dinner starts around 7:30-8pm (or later!). Many restaurants close between lunch and dinner service.

Do I need to book restaurants? +

For popular restaurants, especially on weekends, booking is recommended. Casual trattorias usually don't require reservations.

How much should I tip? +

Tipping isn't expected in Italy like it is in the UK/US. A "coperto" (cover charge) is often included. Rounding up or leaving a few euros for excellent service is appreciated but not required.
