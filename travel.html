Getting There

Your guide to travelling to Tuscany

Nearest Airports

Several airports serve the Tuscany region. Here are your best options.

Florence (FLR)

Recommended

The closest major airport to La Conca. Small, easy to navigate, and well-connected to UK airports.

Distance: ~45 minutes to venue
Airlines: British Airways, Vueling, easyJet

Pisa (PSA)

A popular choice with more budget flight options. Slightly further but easy access via motorway.

Distance: ~1 hour to venue
Airlines: Ryanair, easyJet, British Airways

Rome (FCO)

Italy's largest airport with the most flight options. Consider this if you want to explore more of Italy.

Distance: ~2.5 hours to venue
Airlines: All major carriers

Getting Around

Once you've landed, here's how to get to the venue and explore Tuscany.

Car Hire

Our Recommendation

The best way to explore Tuscany at your own pace. Book in advance for the best rates. All major rental companies are at the airports.

Tip: Italian roads are well-maintained. An automatic car is available but may cost more.

Private Transfer

Sit back and enjoy the scenery. Several companies offer private transfers from the airports to your accommodation.

Tip: Book in advance, especially for larger groups. We can recommend providers on request.

Train + Taxi

Italy's train network is excellent. Take a train to the nearest station, then a short taxi to the venue.

Tip: Book trains via Trenitalia or Italo. First class is affordable and comfortable.

Travel Tips

When should I book flights? +

May is a popular time for Tuscany, so we recommend booking flights as early as possible - ideally 6+ months in advance for the best prices and availability.

Do I need an international driving licence? +

UK licence holders can drive in Italy with their UK licence. Non-EU visitors should check if they need an International Driving Permit (IDP).

Is there parking at the venue? +

Yes! La Conca has ample free parking for guests. If you're staying nearby, the venue is also walkable from many local accommodations.

Should I arrive early or stay late? +

We'd love it if you could! Tuscany is beautiful and there's so much to see. Consider arriving a day or two early to settle in, or staying after for a mini-holiday.

Weather in May

Late May in Tuscany is typically warm and sunny with temperatures around 20-25°C (68-77°F). Pack sunglasses, sunscreen, and a light layer for evenings.

Currency & Payments

Italy uses the Euro (EUR). Credit cards are widely accepted, but smaller shops and trattorias may prefer cash.

Tips:
- Use a fee-free card like Monzo or Starling
- ATMs (Bancomat) are common in towns
- Tipping isn't expected, but appreciated
